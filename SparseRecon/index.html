SparseRecon: Neural Implicit Surface Reconstruction from Sparse Views with Feature and Depth Consistencies

Liang Han1, Haichuan Song2, Kanle Shi3, Yu-Shen Liu1, Zhizhong Han4
1Tsinghua University, 2East China Normal University, 3Kuaishou Technology, 4Wayne State University
Paper Code

Abstract

Surface reconstruction from sparse views aims to reconstruct a 3D shape or scene from few RGB images. However, existing methods are merely limited to working on sparse views with large overlaps, and degenerate severely on views with small overlaps. To address this issue, we propose SparseRecon, a novel neural implicit reconstruction method for sparse views with volume rendering-based feature consistency and calibrated depth constraint. Firstly, we introduce a feature consistency loss across views to constrain the neural implicit field, which alleviates ambiguity caused by insufficient consistency information of views and ensures completeness and smoothness in the reconstruction results. Secondly, we employ a calibrated depth constraint to back up the feature consistency loss in area with large occlusion and insignificant features, which recovers geometry details for better reconstruction quality. Experimental results demonstrate that our method can produce high-quality geometry with sparse-view input, specially in the scenarios on small overlapping views, and outperforms the state-of-the-art methods.
